The 13th ETSF Young Researchers' Meeting

New frontiers in modeling electronic excited-states from confined systems to complex aggregates.
6-10/10/2016 Kings College London, London, UK

The ETSF Young Researchers' Meeting (YRM) is an annual workshop for non-permanent scientists (mainly postdocs and PhD students) working in the field of theoretical electronic structure of real materials. It addresses challenges and recent development in the theory (density-functional, many-body theory, model systems) and in the methods (molecular dynamics, Monte Carlo simulations, code development in general). The YRM is a unique chance for young researchers to present their work to an international audience where scientific discussions are encouraged by a friendly atmosphere.

This year the ETSF YRM will focus on excited states and talks will be divided in five thematic sessions:
  • Strongly correlated electrons
  • Spectroscopy and dielectric properties
  • Modern trends in quantum magnetism
  • Electon-phonon coupling and thermal properties of matter
  • Quantum systems interacting with the environment
Keynote talks at the beginning of each session are intended to provide the participants with an overview of the field, an introduction of the theoretical framework and open questions, and a review of the state-of-the-art methods.

Moreover an entire day will be dedicated to researchers from industry giving the participants the opportunity to discover research outside academia.
